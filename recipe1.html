Perfect Roasted Beef

A mouthwatering, tender and juicy roast that's perfect for any special occasion

Prep: 20 mins
Cook: 1 hr 30 mins
Serves: 6-8

Gideon Nzaka

A Microbiology Student

A Perfect Roast for Any Occasion

This roasted beef recipe combines simple ingredients with precise cooking techniques to create a juicy, tender, and flavorful centerpiece for your dinner table. The outside forms a beautiful crust while the inside remains perfectly pink and juicy.

Low Carb

High Protein

Premium

Ingredients You'll Need

For the Roast

  • 3-4 pound (1.5-2kg) beef tenderloin or ribeye roast
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon coarse sea salt
  • 2 teaspoons freshly ground black pepper

For the Sauce (Optional)

  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1/2 cup red wine
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Chef's Tip

For the best results, let your beef come to room temperature for about 1 hour before cooking. This ensures more even cooking throughout the roast.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Prepare the Beef

Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Pat the beef dry with paper towels. This is important for achieving a good sear.

For a tender roast, look for good marbling (fat distributed throughout the meat) when selecting your beef cut.

2

Season the Roast

In a small bowl, mix together the olive oil, minced garlic, chopped rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper. Rub this mixture all over the beef, making sure to coat it evenly on all sides.

For maximum flavor, you can season the beef up to 24 hours in advance and refrigerate it. Just remember to bring it to room temperature before roasting.

3

Sear the Beef

Heat a large oven-safe skillet over high heat. Once hot, add the beef and sear it on all sides until it develops a deep brown crust, about 2-3 minutes per side.

Don't skip the searing step! This creates a flavorful crust and helps seal in the juices.

4

Roast to Perfection

Transfer the skillet with the beef to the preheated oven. Roast until the internal temperature reaches your desired doneness:

  • Rare: 120-125°F (about 15 minutes per pound)
  • Medium-Rare: 130-135°F (about 20 minutes per pound)
  • Medium: 140-145°F (about 25 minutes per pound)
  • Medium-Well: 150-155°F (about 30 minutes per pound)

A meat thermometer is essential for achieving perfect doneness. Insert it into the thickest part of the roast for an accurate reading.

5

Rest the Meat

Once the beef reaches your desired temperature, remove it from the oven. Transfer to a cutting board, loosely tent with foil, and let it rest for 15-20 minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat.

Do not skip the resting period! It's critical for a juicy roast.

6

Make the Sauce (Optional)

While the beef is resting, prepare the sauce. Using the same skillet with the beef drippings:

  1. Place the skillet over medium heat and add the red wine to deglaze, scraping up the browned bits.
  2. Add the beef broth and simmer for 5 minutes to reduce slightly.
  3. In a small bowl, mix the butter and flour to form a paste.
  4. Whisk the paste into the simmering liquid until it thickens.
  5. Stir in the Dijon mustard and season with salt and pepper to taste.
7

Slice and Serve

Slice the beef against the grain into approximately 1/2-inch thick slices. Arrange on a serving platter and drizzle with the sauce if using. Garnish with fresh herbs if desired.

Serving suggestion: Pair with roasted potatoes, Yorkshire pudding, or seasonal vegetables for a complete meal.

Tips & Variations

Perfect Doneness

For the most accurate results, use a digital meat thermometer. Remember that meat continues to cook during the resting period, so remove it from the oven when it's 5°F below your target temperature.

Flavor Variations

Try these alternative seasonings: black garlic and rosemary; coffee and cocoa rub; or Asian-inspired with ginger, soy, and five-spice powder. Each creates a completely different flavor profile.

Wine Pairings

This roast beef pairs wonderfully with full-bodied red wines like Cabernet Sauvignon, Malbec, or Syrah. The rich flavors of the beef complement the tannins in these bold wines.
